# Learning & Research Kickoff Checklist

Extends `universal-checklist.md`. Asked after domain detection identifies a learning or research project.

---

## Current State

1. **What's your current knowledge level on this topic?** (Zero | Beginner surface awareness | Intermediate working knowledge | Advanced with gaps | Expert seeking depth)
2. **What specifically do you already know?** (Brief summary — helps calibrate depth and avoid re-explaining basics.)
3. **What triggered this research?** (Curiosity | Work requirement | Decision to make | Problem to solve | Skill to learn)
4. **What's the end goal?** (Understand a concept | Make a decision | Build something | Teach someone | Write about it)

## Scope & Depth

5. **Breadth vs. depth?** (Survey of a broad area | Deep dive on a specific subtopic | Both — start broad, then drill)
6. **What's the time commitment?** (2-hour crash course | Multi-day deep dive | Ongoing learning over weeks)
7. **What output do you need?** (Notes for yourself | Summary for others | Article/presentation | Curriculum plan | Mental model/framework | No artifact — just understanding)
8. **Source quality requirements?** (Primary research only | Peer-reviewed + reputable | Any credible source | Informal is fine)

## Specifics

9. **What specific questions do you want answered?** (List them. These become the research agenda.)
10. **Are there sources you already trust on this topic?** (Books, papers, experts, courses, sites)
11. **Are there sources or perspectives you want to AVOID?** (Biased sources, outdated material, specific schools of thought)
12. **What's the biggest gap you're aware of?** (The thing you know you don't know. There will also be gaps you don't know about — identifying those is part of the process.)

---

## Conditional

### If curriculum/learning plan
13. **What's your learning style preference?** (Reading | Video | Hands-on projects | Discussion | Mixed)
14. **What's the schedule?** (Daily time available, total weeks, milestones)
15. **How will you know you've learned it?** (Can explain it | Can build with it | Can teach it | Can pass a test)

### If research synthesis
16. **How many sources should be synthesized?** (Top 3-5 | Comprehensive survey | Everything available)
17. **Is there a specific format for the synthesis?** (Literature review style | Comparative table | Narrative | Annotated bibliography)

### If decision support
18. **What's the decision being informed?** (This shapes which aspects of the research matter most.)
19. **What's the decision deadline?** (Research depth adapts to available time.)
