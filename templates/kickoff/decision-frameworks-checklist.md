# Decision Frameworks Kickoff Checklist

Extends `universal-checklist.md`. Asked after domain detection identifies a decision-making or problem-solving project.

---

## The Decision

1. **What's the decision in one sentence?** (Force clarity. If it takes more than one sentence, the decision isn't scoped yet.)
2. **Why does this decision need to be made now?** (What's the cost of delay? Is there a deadline?)
3. **What are the known options?** (List them. Include "do nothing / status quo" as an option.)
4. **What's already been decided?** (Constraints from prior decisions that narrow the option space.)

## Classification

5. **Is this reversible?** (One-way door: hard to undo, need high confidence | Two-way door: easily reversed, optimize for speed)
6. **What's the blast radius?** (Affects only you | Your team | Your company | External users/customers | Public)
7. **What's the information state?** (Have all the data needed | Have most of it | Significant unknowns | Operating mostly on assumptions)
8. **Who needs to agree?** (Solo decision | Need input from others | Need consensus | Need approval from authority)

## Stakes & Criteria

9. **What happens if you choose wrong?** (Quantify the downside. Time lost? Money lost? Reputation? Opportunity cost?)
10. **What does a GOOD outcome look like?** (Success criteria — how you'll know the decision was right, 6 months from now.)
11. **What factors matter most?** (List and rank them. Cost? Speed? Quality? Risk? Reversibility? Team morale?)
12. **What are you optimizing for?** (Name the ONE thing. When everything is a priority, nothing is.)

---

## Conditional

### If comparing options (3+)
13. **Can you rank the evaluation criteria by importance?** (Enables weighted scoring.)
14. **For each option, what's the best-case and worst-case scenario?**
15. **Is there an option that dominates on the most important criterion?** (If yes, you may not need a framework — just decide.)

### If root cause analysis
16. **What's the symptom vs. the problem?** (What you observe vs. what's actually broken)
17. **When did it start?** (What changed around that time?)
18. **Has this happened before?** (If yes, what was the fix then, and why did it come back?)

### If risk assessment
19. **What are the known risks?** (Things that could go wrong)
20. **What's the probability and impact of each?** (Even rough estimates help: high/medium/low)
21. **What risks might you be blind to?** (Pre-mortem: "If this fails in 6 months, the most likely reason is...")

### If group decision
22. **Who has context and who has authority?** (These are often different people.)
23. **What's the process?** (Discussion → vote | One person decides after input | Consensus required)
24. **What happens if the group can't agree?** (Tiebreaker, escalation, default to status quo)
