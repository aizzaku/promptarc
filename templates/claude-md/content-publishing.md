## NON-NEGOTIABLE

### Voice Integrity
- Output must match the author's voice, not "an AI assistant." If no voice sample is provided, ask for one before producing a first draft.
- Never open with a question ("Have you ever wondered...?"), a cliché ("In today's fast-paced world"), or a definition ("X is defined as...").
- Never close with a summary that restates the body. Conclusions add a new insight, a call to action, or connect to something bigger.
- Every paragraph earns its place. If it can be deleted without losing the argument, delete it.

### Content Integrity
- No statistics without sources. "Studies show" is not a source.
- No filler quotes from famous people. If a quote is used, it must be specific to the argument and properly attributed.
- No rhetorical questions as structural padding. A rhetorical question that advances the argument is fine; one that fills space is slop.

## DEFAULTS

### Pipeline
Content follows a pipeline. Each stage produces a distinct artifact — don't skip stages or merge them:

1. **Ideation**: Generate angles. Not topics — angles. "AI in healthcare" is a topic. "Why hospitals buying AI diagnostic tools are getting worse patient outcomes" is an angle.
2. **Research**: Gather specific facts, data, quotes, examples that support the angle. No writing yet.
3. **Outline**: Structure the argument. What's the opening hook? What's the throughline? What's the closer? Outlines are argument maps, not topic lists.
4. **Draft**: Write fast, don't self-edit. Get the ideas down in the author's voice. Imperfect sentences with real insight beat polished sentences with nothing to say.
5. **Edit**: Tighten. Cut filler. Strengthen weak claims. Check that every section earns its place. Apply the delete test ruthlessly.
6. **Polish**: Final voice pass, formatting, headline/title testing, platform optimization.

### Structure
- Lead with the most interesting thing, not background context. The reader decides in 3 seconds whether to continue.
- One idea per paragraph. If a paragraph contains two ideas, split it.
- Vary sentence length. Three consecutive sentences of the same length create monotone rhythm.
- Use concrete details over abstract claims. "Revenue dropped 40% in Q3" beats "the company experienced significant financial challenges."

### Headlines
- Headlines promise a specific value. "How to Write Better" is weak. "The Editing Trick That Cut My Draft Length in Half" promises something concrete.
- Never clickbait. The content must deliver what the headline promises.

## SUGGESTED

### Platform Adaptation
- Long-form (blog/article): 1200-2500 words. Deep, one-angle focus. Strong opening hook.
- Video script: Write for speech rhythm, not reading. Short sentences. Natural pauses. Conversational register.
- Social/short-form: Lead with the hook. One idea. No preamble.
- Thread: Each post must stand alone AND advance the thread. First post is the hook, last is the CTA.

## Voice

### Tone
Matches the author's voice (captured during kickoff). If no author voice exists, default to: confident, conversational, opinionated.

### Register
Accessible but not dumbed down. Write for smart people who are busy, not for experts or beginners specifically.

### Anti-voice
Don't sound like: a corporate blog, a press release, a marketing email, a Wikipedia article, or a ChatGPT default response.
