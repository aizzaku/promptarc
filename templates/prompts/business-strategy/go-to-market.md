# Pattern: Build a Go-to-Market Strategy

> "I want to build a go-to-market strategy"

**Principles used**: Context Layering, Chain-of-Thought Scaffolding, Specificity Amplification

---

## When to Use
- Launching a new product or entering a new market
- Current GTM isn't working and needs rethinking
- Transitioning from early adopters to broader market

## The Pattern

```
Build a go-to-market strategy for: {{PRODUCT}}

Product: {{WHAT_IT_DOES}}
Target customer: {{SPECIFIC_SEGMENT — not "SMBs" but "B2B SaaS companies with 20-100 employees who currently use spreadsheets for project tracking"}}
Price: {{PRICE_AND_MODEL}}
Current traction: {{WHAT_YOU_HAVE — users, revenue, waitlist, nothing}}
Budget: {{MARKETING/SALES_BUDGET — or "near zero"}}
Team: {{WHO_CAN_EXECUTE — "just me" | "me + 1 marketer" | etc.}}

Build the strategy in layers:
1. First 50 customers: How do you get them individually? (No "content marketing" at this stage — be specific about individual outreach tactics.)
2. First 500 customers: What repeatable channel gets you here? (ONE channel, done well. Not five channels done poorly.)
3. Scaling beyond: What does the growth engine look like? (Product-led? Sales-led? Content flywheel?)

For each layer:
- Specific tactics (not "social media marketing" but "daily posts in 3 specific Slack communities where target customers ask questions")
- Expected conversion rates (with stated assumptions)
- Cost per acquisition estimate
- Timeline to results

Do NOT: Recommend tactics that require a team or budget the user doesn't have. Strategy must match current resources.
```

## Why It Works
The three-layer approach prevents premature scaling advice. Requiring resource-matched tactics ensures the strategy is actually executable. Conversion rate estimates force realism.

## Common Mistakes
- Jumping to scalable channels before validating with initial customers
- Recommending "content marketing" for pre-product-market-fit → months of effort before any signal
- Not matching tactics to budget/team → beautiful strategy nobody can execute
