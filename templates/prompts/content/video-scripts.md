# Pattern: Write a Video Script with Natural Pacing

> "I want to write a video script with natural pacing"

**Principles used**: Role and Perspective Framing, Constraint-Driven Creativity, Few-Shot Calibration

---

## When to Use
- Writing for YouTube, course content, or any spoken-word video format
- Converting written content into a script that sounds natural when spoken
- Need pacing that holds attention through a longer video

## The Pattern

```
Write a video script on: {{TOPIC/ANGLE}}

Format: {{TALKING_HEAD | VOICEOVER_WITH_BROLL | SCREENCAST | ESSAY_STYLE}}
Target length: {{MINUTES}} minutes (~{{MINUTES * 150}} words)
Audience: {{WHO}} — they already know {{BASELINE_KNOWLEDGE}}

Structure:
- Hook (first 15 seconds): Bold claim, surprising fact, or demonstrate the problem
- Re-hook at ~30% mark (where retention typically drops)
- Core content with clear transitions the viewer can follow by audio alone
- Payoff/closer: deliver on the hook's promise, then CTA

Writing rules for spoken word:
- Short sentences. Max 20 words. Most under 12.
- Write for the ear, not the eye. Read it aloud — if you stumble, rewrite.
- Use contractions. "Don't" not "do not." "It's" not "it is."
- Signpost verbally: "Here's the thing..." / "But here's what most people miss..." / "The reason this matters is..."
- No parenthetical asides (can't use parentheses in speech)
- Pause beats: [PAUSE] where emphasis is needed

Include visual cues in brackets: [SHOW: screenshot of X] or [CUT TO: diagram of Y]
```

## Why It Works
The word-per-minute target prevents scripts that are too dense or too thin. Writing rules for spoken word override Claude's default written-prose style. Visual cue brackets make the script producible.

## Common Mistakes
- Writing an article and calling it a script → sounds stilted when spoken
- No re-hook → viewers drop off after the intro
- No visual cues → script is just a wall of text, not a production document

## Variations
- **Tutorial/screencast**: Add "Walk through the steps as if the viewer is following along on their own screen. Narrate what you're doing and why, not just what to click."
- **Short-form (<60s)**: "One idea. One hook. One payoff. No context-setting — jump straight in."
