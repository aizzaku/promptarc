# Pattern: Create Social Content from Long-Form

> "I want to create a social media thread from a long-form piece"

**Principles used**: Constraint-Driven Creativity, Specificity Amplification

---

## When to Use
- Repurposing an article, blog post, or essay into social posts
- Creating a thread (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Threads) from a longer piece
- Extracting the most shareable ideas from existing content

## The Pattern

```
Here's my long-form piece: {{PASTE_ARTICLE_OR_LINK}}

Create a {{PLATFORM}} thread ({{NUM_POSTS}} posts) that:

Post 1 (Hook): The single most surprising or counterintuitive claim from the piece. Must make someone stop scrolling.
Posts 2-{{N-1}}: Each post delivers ONE idea that stands alone (someone could screenshot just this post and it would make sense) AND advances the thread's argument.
Post {{N}}: Payoff + CTA ({{WHAT_YOU_WANT — follow, subscribe, read the full piece, reply}})

Rules:
- Each post is under {{CHAR_LIMIT}} characters
- No "Thread 🧵" or "1/" numbering — let the content speak
- No empty transitions ("Now let's talk about..." / "Moving on...")
- Use the original piece's best examples and data — don't generalize
- The thread should make the reader want to read the full piece, not replace it

Platform-specific:
{{PLATFORM_SPECIFIC_INSTRUCTIONS}}
```

## Why It Works
Requiring each post to stand alone AND advance the thread is a strong creative constraint that eliminates filler posts. Pulling specific examples from the source material keeps the thread grounded.

## Common Mistakes
- Trying to compress the entire article → thread is a shallow summary instead of a curated highlight reel
- Posts that only make sense in sequence → individual posts don't get shared
- No hook in post 1 → nobody reads post 2

## Variations
- **Single post distillation**: "What's the ONE sentence from this piece that would get the most engagement on {{PLATFORM}}? Now write 3 variations of that post."
- **Quote cards**: "Extract 3-5 quotable sentences from this piece that work as standalone images. Each should be under 15 words and make someone think."
- **LinkedIn narrative**: "Turn the key insight into a personal story format. Opening line must be one sentence, max 10 words."
