# Pattern: Synthesize Scattered Information into Structure

> "I want to synthesize scattered information into structure"

**Principles used**: Chain-of-Thought Scaffolding, Specificity Amplification

---

## When to Use
- You have lots of notes, thoughts, or data points that need organizing
- Information from multiple conversations, documents, or research sessions
- Need to turn chaos into a coherent narrative or framework

## The Pattern

```
I have scattered information on {{TOPIC}} that I need to organize.

Here's what I have:
{{PASTE_NOTES_THOUGHTS_DATA — can be messy, that's the point}}

The output needs to serve: {{PURPOSE — decision, presentation, plan, article, understanding}}

Synthesize:
1. Identify the 3-5 key themes in this material. (These become the organizing structure.)
2. Group the information under each theme. Flag any information that doesn't fit neatly — these outliers are often the most interesting.
3. For each theme: What's the one-sentence insight? (Not a summary — the takeaway that the raw material implies but doesn't state.)
4. What's the meta-narrative? How do the themes connect to tell a coherent story?
5. What's missing? Given the themes and purpose, what information would strengthen this if I had it?

Output format: {{STRUCTURED_DOCUMENT | OUTLINE | FRAMEWORK_DIAGRAM | TABLE}}

Do NOT: Force every piece of information into the structure. Some things don't fit — note them separately rather than jamming them where they don't belong.
```

## Why It Works
Starting with theme identification (bottom-up) instead of imposing a structure (top-down) lets the material's natural organization emerge. The "meta-narrative" step lifts the synthesis beyond categorization into genuine insight.
