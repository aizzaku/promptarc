# Pattern: Brainstorm Ideas Without Going Generic

> "I want to brainstorm ideas without going generic"

**Principles used**: Constraint-Driven Creativity, Negative Examples, Specificity Amplification

---

## When to Use
- Need creative ideas for any domain
- Past brainstorming sessions produced obvious or generic suggestions
- Want quantity AND quality (not just one of the two)

## The Pattern

```
Brainstorm {{WHAT}} for {{CONTEXT}}.

Constraints:
{{LIST_2_4_SPECIFIC_CONSTRAINTS — these drive creativity, not limit it}}

Generate 10 ideas. Rules:
- Ideas 1-3: The obvious ones. Get them out of the way — we both know these exist. Label them "[OBVIOUS]".
- Ideas 4-7: Non-obvious. These require combining elements that don't normally go together, or applying a concept from an adjacent domain.
- Ideas 8-10: Wild. These might not work, but they'd be interesting if they did. They should make me think "huh, I wouldn't have considered that."

For each idea:
- The idea in one sentence
- Why it might work (one sentence)
- The biggest risk (one sentence)

Do NOT: Pad weak ideas with long explanations. A bad idea with a good explanation is still a bad idea.
```

## Why It Works
The three tiers (obvious → non-obvious → wild) prevent Claude from stopping at the first plausible ideas. Labeling the obvious ones explicitly clears them out rather than pretending they're creative.
